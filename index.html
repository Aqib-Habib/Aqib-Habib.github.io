Thesis & Research

Research work with engineering depth

Focused on fault detection, control, estimation, autonomy, and simulation for aerial robotic systems.

01

Fault Detection Strategies for an Over-Actuated Multicopter

Observer-based residual generation for actuator fault detection under nominal, faulty, and uncertain model conditions.

  • Luenberger, Thau, EKF, and UKF observer comparison
  • Residual thresholds and actuator effectiveness-loss scenarios
  • MATLAB/Simulink-based multicopter dynamics and detection pipeline
02

Geometric Quadrotor Control on SE(3)

Full-state tracking controller for aggressive quadrotor motion using position, velocity, attitude, and angular-rate feedback.

  • Controller implementation in a ROS2/Unity simulation stack
  • Actuator mapping and dynamic trajectory tracking
  • Integration with waypoint and minimum-snap trajectory generation
03

GNSS-Denied UAV Autonomy

End-to-end autonomous navigation stack for GPS-denied environments with mapping, perception, and state estimation.

  • Frontier-based exploration and OctoMap environment representation
  • EKF-based state estimation under noisy sensor conditions
  • Finite-state mission logic for takeoff, cave entry, navigation, and return
