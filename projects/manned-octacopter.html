01Project overview

Designing flight-control logic around a human-carrying safety case.

The GoAero challenge centres on compact, rapidly deployable aircraft that can reach places where roads and runways cannot. For a manned multicopter, control decisions must be made with explicit attention to redundancy, degraded operation and safe test progression.

8×

Redundant propulsion

Eight actuators create additional control-allocation options, but also a larger space of nominal and degraded cases to evaluate.

H

Human mission

Emergency-response scenarios put safety margins, predictable behaviour and verification discipline ahead of spectacle.

03Control & test approach

Build confidence in layers before expanding the envelope.

Model

Represent vehicle dynamics, actuator geometry and achievable force/moment combinations.

Allocate

Distribute control demand across eight propulsors while respecting constraints.

Validate

Exercise nominal and safety-relevant cases in MATLAB UAV Toolbox workflows.

Integrate

Move verified logic toward Pixhawk hardware and controlled flight-test activity.

Safety principleRedundancy is useful only when the controller understands what authority remains. Allocation and degraded-mode thinking therefore belong to the control architecture—not as an afterthought during testing.
