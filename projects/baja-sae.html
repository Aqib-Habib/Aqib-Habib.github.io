01Project overview

A vehicle project where engineering decisions meet mud, deadlines and people.

BAJA SAE asks a student team to operate like a small vehicle company: define a product, justify its engineering and business case, manufacture it, test it and perform under competition scrutiny.

ENG

Technical challenge

Balance structural strength, suspension behaviour, powertrain performance, mass, manufacturability and serviceability.

OPS

Team challenge

Coordinate 60 people across subteams, facilities, procurement, documentation, assembly, testing and competition logistics.

02My contribution

Technical direction and team leadership through the complete build.

01

Engineering Leadership

Led a 60-member multidisciplinary organisation as Team Captain and Chief Engineer, connecting subsystem work to vehicle-level priorities.

02

Vehicle-System Coordination

Coordinated chassis, suspension and powertrain development, including CAD and MATLAB/Simulink performance work.

03

Engineering Definition

Produced and reviewed manufacturing drawings with GD&T so design intent survived the transition to the workshop.

04

Fabrication & Assembly

Supported hands-on production using CNC milling, turning, laser cutting and welding through final assembly.

03Development process

Run the technical programme and the team programme together.

Architect

Set vehicle targets and divide responsibility across chassis, suspension, powertrain and operations.

Design & review

Use CAD, calculations, simulation and cross-subteam reviews to mature interfaces.

Build & resolve

Manufacture parts, manage fit-up issues and keep drawings aligned with practical changes.

Test & compete

Validate vehicle behaviour, prepare presentations and execute at national and international events.

Leadership principleThe captain’s job was not to make every design decision. It was to make interfaces visible, maintain a shared vehicle-level objective and help subteams reach decisions in time to build.

05Results

A functioning vehicle, an international result and a stronger team.

Team Markhor achieved fourth place in the BAJA SAE Arizona Global Sales Presentation and second place overall at the National Piston Cup Challenge. The programme also created repeatable design, documentation and manufacturing experience across a large student team.
